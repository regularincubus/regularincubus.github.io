the way she stands, far more closed off than she ever did. her expression; the tension in her face and the quick, rather shallow breaths. you see her. and it makes you realize just how good you’ve known her all along. just how well you can read her. and it scares you as much as it amazes you.

although this thought did little to calm you down. since furina was not in charge of fontaine, this meant that the flood had already happened. which in return meant that, as of right now, project stuzha must be in preparation. and that made your stomach turn.

“i know about clervie.” you state. her breath hitched just slightly; perfectly composed, but if this arle had one weakness, it was her past.

 

“and how would you know?” she asked in return. “this was a matter in which only the members of the house of the hearth were involved, which makes me question just what role you play in all of this.” she says, trying to keep her composure. you could feel it, could hear how that certain facade faltered just slightly.

 

you feel satisfied to be able to make her feel that way. and it hurts because your arle has dropped that same facade entirely.

 

“i’m not talking about the occurrence at the house” you add quietly. “i could see it. somewhat, at least. how you killed crucabena. the withering lumidouce bell. your curse-” you choked on held back tears as realization hits you. this is the first time you were so close to her curse, and there was next to nothing you could do about it. you couldn’t caress it, stare at it for long. no matter how this conversation finds its end; it would still take ages for arle to open up like that. and that is, if you stayed here, which was just about as certain as clervie coming back to life.

you’re real. so is she.

 

“it’s a long story.” you say. telling her the full truth, explaining to her just how you know all this; telling her about what exactly she is, what teyvat is and how it exists does not sound all too appealing.

 

“essentially, however, somewhere along the way, a part of you ended up with me. in my universe, i mean. trapped in an object.”

 

you know just how absurd that sounds. and maybe this sounds like something one of the children would tell her; one of the younger ones, whose fantasy is still untainted by the reality of it all. and yet, when you look at her again, you find nothing but those damn eyes, staring at the ground. her head was spinning, that much was clear. with what, you could only guess, but you also knew that you wouldn’t want to be in her stead right now. 

 

“my consciousness has split, you say? and traversed the laws of this world, at least those obvious to us, to then find its way to you.” she states and you can only nod. she walks back to her chair again and sits down. she looks at you, into your eyes, and you see softness. how you always envisioned your arle to look at you in such moments; how you saw her whenever you held the pillow close and closed your eyes.

 

she swallows.

 

“it is true that i have been feeling a certain way for some time now.” she starts. “and yet, i could have never imagined this persisting emptiness to be caused through such a thing. it is a miracle, truly. especially now, that i had the pleasure of meeting you, i feel a sense of familiarity, yet i cannot exactly describe it.” 

 

“you don’t need to. i am, you see-  your other self and i are in a relationship.” you state and are met with silence.

 

“most interesting. for you and, well, me, i suppose, to share such a bond despite the hindrances.” she says quietly. a deep inhale. you look at her, she exhales. 

 

she seems spent. confused. even more tired than when she spent a night at a shore, gazing at the stars and the seas. she meets your gaze.

 

you knew she had questions. but she wasn’t stupid; well aware that your time here was limited. that you could disappear within the blink of an eye, and so she took the information and stored it. part of you already knew that it would stay at a place, where it would never be dug up ever again.

 

you’re grateful. wouldn’t want her reality to be all too different after all. and yet, the thought of it hurt.

 

“do you know why you’re here?” she asks quietly. you nod, closing your eyes and letting a tear escape. it makes its way over your cheek, finding refuge at your jaw.

 

“in my world, i saw you. with your colleague. columbina.” the name leaves a bitter taste in your mouth. “and i could not take it anymore.” you confess. and she nodded in return, because why would she ever confess if anything confuses her.

 

she was so stupid. an idiot. here even more so than back at home. but you loved her nonetheless.

“why is that?” she asks. the concern in her voice is maddening. addicting. so is the softness, and yet the deep tone and -

 

she was everything. will forever stay everything. and right now to had the chance to prove to her just how much you adore and worship and love her. and yet, not a single thing came out of your mouth. instead, you collected yourself quickly.

 

“in my world, most people adore other peoples’ relationships. they root for them, wish for them, grieve and are happy with them. however, oftentimes they also want two people to be together and are rather… i’ll be honest, they’re annoying about it. constantly changing the personalities or even appearances to make people fit better.” 

 

the urge to say ‘character’ is strong. luckily, arle doesn’t seem to notice.

 

“and with you it makes me-” were you really going to be vulnerable with her? despite her and your arle being the same, some aspects still felt unfamiliar. and yet; “it makes me furious. or sad. depends on the day. seeing you with columbina, it destroyed me internally.”

 

“how come?” she asked calmly and her cluelessness hurts you in ways you couldn’t even think of prior to this meeting; prior to this unforeseen encounter that leaves your heart a throbbing mess. “what kind of business between me and her could have you so upset?” 

 

you hated how calm she was, how warm she sounded; how she was so painfully like your arle. hated how it confirmed everything she has ever told you; that she was real.

 

and for a second, you wanted all this to stop. for it to be over, to break up with her and get it over with. but you knew you could never do it. she is your person after all.

 

“would it sound somewhat reasonable when i tell you that you standing next to her gets to me?” you say quietly, your voice beginning to strain from the emotions you kept hidden with expertise. “i could see you smiling when you were with her. not genuinely, but the smile of this facade you use when talking on diplomatic terms-” and you just realized how straight up weird all of that must sound. and yet, there was not an ounce of judgement in her gaze. “-but what the people make of it, i; it destroys me.” you confess. 

 

“they assume all kinds of things. where i’m from, you and her are both versions of some old theatric play. and in said play, your characters are in a relationship. you both are so popular amongst the masses, it sickens me. everyone accepts it, no one questions it, and one wrong word from either of you and everyone goes crazy.

 

if being like this could get you answers out of her, then you would gladly lose your dignity, your composure and just about anything else. for her, her alone and always her.

 

“the game you played? in which you scared one another by sneaking up behind the others’ back? i have seen so many romantic things about that. and every single time i wanted to carve my eyeballs out, discard you and just cry for hours. even kill myself, if it ever got too severe.”

 

nothing ever broke you. you have survived far more than most humans and struggled with the aftermath still. and yet, nothing has ever made you as painfully angry as your arle being shipped with columbina. and that you hated the latter for that was also an odd thought. you knew your fair share, so it might be a little unfair to hate her.

 

but how could you not? if everyone projected their stupidly sick fantasies onto both of them; if everyone acted like they had kissed on screen.

 

they did not even talk just yet.

did not have flirty moments, did not have their own short film, nothing. it was just the play and nostalgia that kept the people. and how arle was portrayed; tall, muscular, utterly handsome and the perfect husband.

 

you feld like throwing up. seeing her in front of you, in flesh and blood; how could this be a  perfect husband? when she was your perfect wife already?

 

“so please, arlecchino,” even that name feels slightly sour in your mouth right now; your thoughts racing with all those delusional people. “please tell me there is nothing between you both. please.”

 

silence. 

 

of course you’re met with silence. and yet, quicker than anticipated, the click of her heels brings you back to life. 

 

suddenly, she stands before you. you look up at her, and under any normal circumstance, your gaze would have lingered on her for some more time. and yet, when you felt a singular finger of hers at your chin, lifting your head up, your heart skipped two beats before stopping entirely.

 

“i cannot name that feeling exactly, yet the thought of you has never failed to not amaze me. i do not know what my other self and you share. and though i find myself quite curious indeed, i don’t know if i truly wish to know.” 

 

her hand withdraws again and you feel like an abandoned kitten.

 

“but i can assure you that the sentiment those people see in her and me, is nothing more than a mere exaggeration of things as they are.”

 

the sound of her voice was warm. wrapped around you like a blanket, and for the first time in weeks, you felt safe.

 

“you know about clervie. so you probably know about the bond we shared. and that is all there is to it.” 

 

her voice broke, her eyes dimmed. seeing her being hurt makes you as happy as it concerns you; the sheer want to pull her in and comfort her, finally really embracing her like you had planned for so long is slowly taking over our very soul. and yet you stayed silent.

“so, vanessa, i can reassure you that between her and me is merely a collegial relationship. and, admittedly, with her leaving the ranks of the fatui, even that denotation proves to be incorrect.” she said.

 

you stared at her. your heart didn’t feel lighter, realizing this was nowhere close to being enough. you needed her, in all the ways. needed to pull her in, bury your face in her chest, feeling the warmth of her body against yours. you needed to touch her, to kiss her; needed to fuck her and stay connected for all eternity.

 

and yet, all you could manage to do is speak.

 

“arle-” you tried not to care about the nickname slipping out. “your other self, she told me something. something so silly, it will ruin this moment for sure.”

 

“oh?” she said, her eyes hardening again just slightly. “do tell.”

 

you took a deep breath. cursed yourself for what you were about to say.

 

“you were 13. there was a cucumber involved. need i say more?”

 

her eyes widen and her mouth turn to open, before she ultimately composes herself and goes back to her strict demeanour again. however, she does not speak. not a single word. merely stared ad you, clearly considering and planning her next move. and yet, she felt it too. this connection, this trust, maybe even this love. she felt it deeply, so there was no denying.

 

“i suppose not. and i must say, i admire the trust between me and you.” she said. “i obviously haven’t told anyone about this, so, for you to know such a secret is a little unexpected. though not unpleasant.”

 

you stare at her, a huge smile starting to form. this was basically all the confirmation you needed and honestly? this was so much more than you could have ever wished for.

darkness engulfed you. this feeling in your heart, heavy and throbbing. it was painful this time, beyond anything you have ever felt. but at the same time, there was a lightness to it; as if something within you had finally clicked, after all this time.

you met arle. the real arle. your arle, or rather the part of her that has been missing for so long. a wave of joy overcomes you; so much, that you don’t even process her words about a certain other harbinger. that will follow in the morning.

 

for now, you close your eyes. you feel. the blanket around your body, maulis arm in your hand, your arle in your arms.
